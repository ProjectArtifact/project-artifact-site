What is Project Artifact?

Project Artifact is a graphic novel set in a near-future world ruled by megacorporations and shaped by emerging technologies. In the year 2257, Dr. Gideon Gates uses an AI-powered research tool to uncover disturbing truths about humanity's forgotten past. His discovery shakes the foundations of science, faith, and corporate control, leading him on a journey that could change the future—if he survives the fallout.

About the Author

Trey Bowling is a writer, storyteller, and creative leader based in Texas. With a background in multimedia communication and a passion for sci-fi grounded in theology and truth, he creates worlds that explore the tension between discovery and belief. Project Artifact is his debut graphic novel.

About the Artist

Daniele Aquilani is an Italian illustrator known for his dynamic characters, expressive linework, and cinematic layouts. His portfolio spans comics, concept art, and book covers. With Project Artifact, he brings a gritty elegance and emotional weight to the story’s cyber-dystopian vision.

FAQ

Is Project Artifact appropriate for all ages?

The graphic novel is best suited for ages 13 and up due to thematic elements, some violence, and mature subject matter.

Will there be more issues?

Yes. The first volume is the beginning of a larger story arc. Future volumes will expand the world and characters further.

Is the story based on real history?

Project Artifact is fictional but inspired by historical mysteries, archeology, and a biblical worldview.
